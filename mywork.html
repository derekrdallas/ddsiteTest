Teaching:

I teach the following courses at Parkland College in the CSIT department in the Digital Media Program where I am a full time tenured faculty.

    CSC179 Digital Media Foundations:
    In this introduction to digital Media we survey a variety of tools to create digital images and video games. Topics include: Photoshop basics, Processing basics, Game Maker basics. Through this course students learn first hand the kind of tools and techniques used to create digital images and video games. It is s a great way to get the newbie or curious student introduced to the field of digital media.

    CSC186 2D Animation:
    In this introductory course we survey the history of animation and learn how to create animation in a wide variety of ways starting with hand drawn, line animation and ending by creating an instructional video utilizing a multi-media approach that allows the students to put to use all of the ideas we discuss throughout the course. The goal is to expose the students to the wide variety of animation techniques, the artists who pioneered the art form and to get them to try out as many different animation methods that I can.

    CSC233: Animation Scripting
    In this course students learn the Processing language to create animation and interactive digital media projects and MEL (Maya Embedded Language) MEL is a scripting language utilized in the Autodesk 3D Modeling and Animation software called MAYA. Students learn to expand Mayas current capabilities by creating custom tools that currently do not exist in the software.

    CSC294 Portfolio:
    In this course, senior students put to work all their skills in our real world working studio. A variety of skills are needed as the students work in a production pipeline to complete projects by deadline. Each student is required to work no-less than 15 hours per week on the projects. Once a student completes their task a new assignment is given and the work begins again.

Freelance Work:
My work is usually in Animation or motion Graphics. I tend to lean towards Motion Graphics over other things.
However, I am currently focusing on Industrial design and website design. Below are some projects I have been involved with over time.

Fortress of Anu: Motion Graphic
This all began with a simple black and white image and the client wanted to create something interesting for the website. I designed and created this motion graphic basically all on my own to demonstrate the power of utilizing 3D animation to create a compelling and engaging animation.

Safety Zone:Motion Graphics and Character Animation
In this project I created all the opening animation and some basic character animation for the female character. For the game screen animation I used after effects and for the character animation I used Maya. This project went on to earn a Telly Award for Heroic Age Studios.
